📐 Calculate monthly interest with basic maths

You don't need a spreadsheet. The formula has three steps:

    1. Daily rate = Annual rate ÷ 365
    e.g. 6.14% ÷ 365 = 0.01682% per day

    2. Monthly interest = Daily rate × Days in month × Effective balance
    e.g. 0.0001682 × 30 × $500,000 = $2,523

    3. Effective balance = Loan balance − Total offset funds
    e.g. $520,000 loan − $20,000 offset = $500,000

Shortcut: multiply your loan balance by (rate% ÷ 1200) to get approximate monthly interest at 30 days.
$500,000 × (6.14 ÷ 1200) = $2,558/month

⚖️ Offset vs redraw — key difference

Offset funds are legally yours at all times. Redraw funds sit inside the loan — the bank can restrict access during financial hardship. For salary cycling, always use an offset account. Reserve redraw for large one-off lump sums you won't need to access quickly.

🏦 Multiple offset accounts

Each offset account reduces your interest-bearing balance dollar-for-dollar. Splitting funds across purpose-built accounts — bills buffer, emergency fund, everyday spending — keeps your budget organised while every dollar still offsets the loan. There's usually no penalty for having several.

💸 Salary cycling (high-impact strategy)

Park your entire salary into your offset account the moment it lands. Pay all expenses via a low-fee credit card, then clear the card in full on the due date. Your salary sits in offset for the maximum number of days each month, slashing the effective balance — and the interest — for that entire period.

📈 Modelling RBA rate changes

On a variable loan, each 0.25% rate rise costs roughly $1,700–$1,900/yr in extra interest on a $700k balance. Each 0.25% cut saves the same. Update the rate field here whenever your bank notifies you of a change to immediately see the impact on your payoff date and total interest cost.

⏩ Fortnightly vs monthly repayments

Paying fortnightly (26 payments/yr) instead of monthly (12 payments/yr) is equivalent to making one extra full monthly repayment each year. On a 30-year $600k loan at 6%, this alone can cut roughly 3–4 years off your loan and save tens of thousands in interest — with no change to your lifestyle spend.

🔁 Lump sum vs extra monthly — which wins?

A lump sum (tax return, bonus, inheritance) reduces your principal immediately and the interest saving compounds from day one. A steady extra monthly repayment builds more slowly but is often easier to sustain. If you receive a windfall, apply it directly to the loan or offset before spending — you can always redraw if genuinely needed.
